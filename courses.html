Courses & Training

Looking to build your skills in organic growing? These programmes and courses offer hands‑on experience and guidance. Always check providers for availability and fees.

Soil Association Future Growers

Provider: Soil Association

This programme pairs trainees with established organic farms for a season, offering practical work alongside study days. It’s ideal for people who want to run a market garden or farm shop.

Programme details

Permaculture Design Certificate

Provider: Various UK tutors

The PDC is a 72‑hour course covering the principles of permaculture design – a holistic approach to growing food, building soil and caring for the land. Courses run around the UK and combine classroom teaching with site visits.

Find a course

FarmStart (Kindling Trust)

Provider: Kindling Trust – Greater Manchester

FarmStart helps new entrants launch small organic farms. Trainees learn field skills, business planning and co‑operative working while growing crops on shared land.

More information

Organic Growers Alliance Apprenticeship

Provider: Organic Growers Alliance

This apprenticeship places trainees on organic market gardens for a full growing season. It combines work‑based learning with study days on crop planning, soil health and organic certification.
